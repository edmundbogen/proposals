Agreement Terms

Term

Initial term: 6 months from signing. Auto-renews month-to-month unless either party gives 30 days written notice. Annual fee increases, after the Founding Partner 24-month lock expires, capped at the greater of CPI (trailing 12-month) or 10% with 60 days written notice.

Intellectual Property

All custom deliverables are works made for hire. Full ownership transfers to Client upon receipt of final payment. To the extent any deliverable is deemed not a work made for hire, Service Provider irrevocably assigns all right, title, and interest to Client upon final payment. Service Provider retains all rights in pre-existing tools, frameworks, and methodologies, and grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free license to use them solely as incorporated in the delivered website. Service Provider retains the right to display the completed site as portfolio and marketing material.

Warranty

60-day post-launch warranty. Service Provider will correct any material defect — defined as a failure of the delivered work to substantially conform to the specifications agreed during the design review phase — at no additional cost, provided Client notifies Service Provider in writing with reasonable specificity.

THIS WARRANTY IS EXCLUSIVE AND REPLACES ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

Warranty does not cover defects caused by Client modifications, third-party services, or Client-provided content.

Guarantee Conditions

The 14-Day Launch Guarantee and First Lead Guarantee on Page 2 apply only when: (i) Client provides complete content within 10 business days of kickoff; (ii) Client responds to review requests within 5 business days; and (iii) no force majeure event occurs. "Complete content" means all text, logos, images, bios, contact info, and brand assets needed to build the agreed scope. A "qualified lead" is a form submission with valid contact info and stated interest (buying, selling, or information request), OR a phone call from a non-spam contact made specifically through the website's contact number. Excludes spam, test submissions, bot traffic, and internal team activity.

Liability

Each party's total liability is capped at the greater of 12 months of fees paid or $10,000. Neither party is liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or lost-profit damages. Exceptions: willful misconduct, confidentiality breaches, IP infringement, and indemnification obligations.

Indemnification (Mutual)

Each party will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other from third-party claims arising out of (a) its own breach of this Agreement, (b) its own willful misconduct or gross negligence, (c) IP infringement caused by content or instructions it provided, and (d) violations of applicable law for which it is responsible. Indemnification obligations survive termination for two (2) years and are not subject to the liability cap.

Insurance

Service Provider maintains Errors & Omissions insurance and commercially reasonable coverage appropriate to the scope of services. Certificate available upon request.

Confidentiality & Data

Both parties keep confidential information private for 3 years after termination (trade secrets survive longer). Client Data remains Client's property — Service Provider will not sell, share, or use it for unrelated purposes. Data Breach notification within 48 hours of discovery. Upon termination, Service Provider exports all Client Data in machine-readable format within 15 days and permanently deletes copies within 30 days thereafter.

Termination

For convenience: Either party, 30 days written notice after Initial Term. For cause: Immediate notice with 15-day cure period for material breach. On termination: Client receives all website files, databases, and Client Data within 15 days of final payment; Service Provider provides up to 15 business days of transition assistance at $150/hr if requested in writing within 30 days of termination notice.

Disputes

Florida governing law. Good-faith negotiation (15 days), then binding arbitration administered by JAMS via remote/virtual proceedings unless both parties agree otherwise in writing. Nominal seat: Palm Beach County, Florida. Small claims under $5,000 exempted. Class action waiver. Either party may seek emergency injunctive relief in court for IP matters. Each party bears its own attorneys' fees and costs unless the arbitrator finds a claim was frivolous.

Client Responsibilities

Client holds a valid Colorado real estate license, is responsible for compliance with Fair Housing, MLS rules, NAR Code of Ethics, and Colorado licensing requirements, and is solely responsible for the accuracy and legality of all content provided to Service Provider, including property descriptions, photographs, and testimonials.

General

Service Provider is an independent contractor. This Agreement is the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior discussions, proposals, drafts, and agreements. Agreement may be signed electronically (E-SIGN / Florida UETA). Force majeure provisions apply. If any provision is invalid, the remainder continues in effect.

This Agreement does not constitute legal, financial, or tax advice. Each party should consult qualified professionals.
