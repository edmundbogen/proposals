ListingStory
Service Agreement

Between Dave T Productions, LLC d/b/a Bogen.ai and Carrie McCormick · Draft v1 · July 24, 2026

This ListingStory Service Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the date of last signature below (the "Effective Date") by and between Dave T Productions, LLC, a Florida limited liability company doing business as Bogen.ai ("Provider"), and Carrie McCormick, an individual real estate licensee affiliated with @properties Christie's International Real Estate ("Client"). Provider and Client are each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

1.Definitions

  • "Platform" means Provider's ListingStory software system, including all code, templates, page layouts, designs, prompts, workflows, integrations, documentation, and improvements, whether existing before or developed during this Agreement.
  • "Client Instance" means the Client-branded deployment of the Platform operated by Provider for Client.
  • "Client Data" means information submitted to the Client Instance by Client, Client's team, or property sellers, including seller contact information and property information.
  • "Client Brand" means Client's name, likeness, logos, trademarks, brand assets, and biographical content.
  • "Generated Content" means text, documents, pages, and reports produced by the Client Instance for Client (e.g., Property Stories, feature sheets, listing-page content, analytics reports), excluding the Platform's templates, layouts, designs, and code.
  • "AI Services" means artificial-intelligence models and services selected by Provider, which may include services from Anthropic, OpenAI, or other vendors. Provider may select, combine, and substitute AI Services and other subprocessors at its sole discretion, provided the quality of the Services is not materially degraded.
  • "Order Form" means a scope-and-fees exhibit executed by both Parties under this Agreement. Exhibit A (Module 1) is the initial Order Form. Additional modules require a new Order Form; informal communications (including email or text) do not modify scope.

2.Services

2.1 Provider will build, host, operate, and support the Client Instance and deliver the services described in each executed Order Form (the "Services").

2.2 Change orders. Scope changes, additional features, and new modules are added only by a written Order Form or amendment signed by both Parties.

2.3 Non-exclusivity. The Services are provided on a non-exclusive basis. Provider may develop, market, and provide the same or similar services, systems, and content frameworks to other clients, provided Provider will never use Client Brand or Client Data for any other client. Any territorial exclusivity, if agreed, will be set out in a separately priced addendum.

2.4 Service levels. Provider will perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner and will use commercially reasonable efforts to meet the target turnaround times described in an Order Form. Target times are goals, not guarantees, and are conditioned on Client Dependencies (Section 6) and Third-Party Services (Section 9). No service credits apply.

3.Fees & Payment

3.1 Fees for each module are stated in its Order Form. For Module 1: a one-time build fee of $3,500 (50% deposit on signature of this Agreement; 50% on Acceptance as defined in Exhibit A) and a monthly service fee of $395 beginning on the Acceptance date, billed monthly in advance.

3.2 Method. Provider invoices via Stripe; payment by ACH or card. Client authorizes automatic monthly charge of recurring fees to a payment method on file.

3.3 Late payment. Amounts unpaid ten (10) days past due accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum lawful rate, whichever is less. Provider may suspend the Services (including hosting, sync, and generation) on five (5) days' written notice until cured. Suspension is not termination and does not relieve accrued fees.

3.4 Fee changes. Recurring fees are fixed for the Initial Term of the applicable Order Form and may be adjusted thereafter on thirty (30) days' written notice. Recurring fees do not vary with Client's listing volume, subject to the fair-use assumption in the applicable Order Form.

3.5 Cancellation during build. If Client terminates for convenience before Acceptance of a module, the deposit for that module is non-refundable as compensation for work performed, and deliverables completed as of termination are provided to Client under Section 5.

3.6 Taxes. Fees are exclusive of applicable taxes, which are Client's responsibility (excluding taxes on Provider's income).

4.Term & Termination

4.1 Term. This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues while any Order Form is active. Each Order Form runs for an initial term of twelve (12) months from its Acceptance date (the "Initial Term"), then renews month-to-month.

4.2 Termination for convenience. After the Initial Term, either Party may terminate an Order Form or this Agreement on thirty (30) days' written notice.

4.3 Termination for cause. Either Party may terminate if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within fifteen (15) days of written notice. Provider may terminate immediately for non-payment continuing ten (10) days after a suspension under Section 3.3.

4.4 Effect of termination. On termination: (a) the Client Instance and listing pages are taken offline; (b) Provider exports Client Data and Generated Content per Section 8.5; (c) each Party ceases use of the other's marks except archival/portfolio use under Section 13; (d) accrued payment obligations survive. Sections 5, 8, 10–12, and 14–16 survive termination.

5.Intellectual Property & Ownership

Plain-language summary (non-operative): Client owns her data, her brand, and everything the system produces for her. Provider owns the engine.

5.1 Platform. Provider owns the Platform, including all improvements made during this Agreement. No Platform rights transfer to Client. Nothing in any proposal or communication shall be construed as transferring ownership of the Platform, source code, templates, or prompts.

5.2 Client Data and Client Brand. Client owns Client Data and Client Brand. Client grants Provider a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use them during the Term solely to provide the Services (including display of Client Brand on the Client Instance and listing pages), consistent with the Illinois Right of Publicity Act where applicable.

5.3 Generated Content. As between the Parties, Client owns Generated Content upon payment of the fees due for the applicable period. "Generated Content" excludes the Platform's templates, page designs, layouts, and code, which remain Provider's.

5.4 Client Instance. The Client Instance is licensed to Client, not sold, for the duration of an active subscription.

6.Client Responsibilities & Dependencies

6.1 Review and approval. Client (or her designee) must review and approve all Generated Content before publication or distribution. Client is solely responsible for the content she approves, publishes, or distributes, including its accuracy and its compliance with fair-housing laws, MLS rules, advertising regulations, and her brokerage's policies. The Platform's screening tools support, but do not replace, Client's review.

6.2 Brokerage acknowledgment. Before the first listing publishes through the Client Instance, Client will obtain written acknowledgment of her use of the Services from her managing broker. Provider will supply a summary document for this purpose.

6.3 Dependencies. Client will provide in a timely manner: Monday.com account access, brand assets and bio/team copy, property photographs, seller cooperation, draft approvals, and scheduled calls. Provider's target timelines are extended day-for-day by delays in Client Dependencies.

6.4 Seller-facing terms. Client will direct sellers to complete the intake truthfully and will not remove or bypass the intake's privacy notice, consent, and accuracy-attestation steps.

7.AI Disclaimer

7.1 Generated Content is produced with AI Services and is provided as drafts for Client's review. Provider does not warrant that Generated Content is accurate, complete, compliant, or fit for publication without review. Automated screening (including the fair-housing word filter) is a support tool and does not constitute legal advice or a compliance guarantee.

7.2 Provider does not guarantee business results, including listing volume, sale outcomes, page traffic, or lead generation.

7.3 Provider is a technology and consulting provider, not a law firm and not a real estate brokerage, and provides no legal or brokerage services under this Agreement.

8.Data, Privacy & Security

8.1 Use. Provider uses Client Data solely to provide the Services. Provider will not sell Client Data and will not use seller personal information for Provider's own marketing.

8.2 Subprocessors. Client authorizes the subprocessors listed in Exhibit B (AI, hosting, database, email, and spreadsheet services). Provider may update Exhibit B by notice, provided replacements are subject to materially similar protections.

8.3 Security. Provider will implement and maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data, consistent with the Illinois Personal Information Protection Act (815 ILCS 530), including access controls on administrative dashboards and encryption in transit.

8.4 Sensitive access information. The intake is designed so that property access details (e.g., codes) are not collected through the form; sellers are directed to convey any such information to Client directly. Client will not instruct sellers otherwise.

8.5 Export and deletion. On termination or written request, Provider will export Client Data and Generated Content in commercially standard formats (CSV/JSON and document files) within fifteen (15) days, and will delete Client Data from active systems within sixty (60) days of a written deletion request, except as retained in routine backups (purged on cycle) or as required by law.

8.6 Breach. Each Party will notify the other without undue delay upon becoming aware of a breach of security affecting Client Data and will reasonably cooperate on required notifications.

9.Third-Party Services

The Services depend on third-party services not controlled by Provider, including Monday.com, AI Services vendors, hosting, database, and email providers. Provider is not liable for their acts, omissions, outages, price changes, or API changes, and may substitute providers under Section 1 ("AI Services") and Section 8.2. Client's use of Monday.com is governed by Client's own agreement with Monday.com.

Client will defend and indemnify Provider against third-party claims arising from (a) content Client approved, published, or distributed; (b) Client's real estate activities and brokerage obligations; (c) Client Data supplied by Client or her sellers; or (d) Client's breach of Section 6.

Each Party may identify the other by name and logo as a client/provider, and Provider may describe the engagement in its portfolio and marketing (excluding Client Data and non-public terms). Case studies quoting Client require Client's prior written approval, not to be unreasonably withheld.

14.Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for delay or failure caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including internet or third-party service outages, acts of God, labor disputes, or governmental action, provided the affected Party resumes performance promptly.

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to conflicts rules. Before filing suit, the Parties will attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through direct negotiation and, failing that, non-binding mediation. This Agreement, with its Order Forms and exhibits, is the entire agreement and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous proposals, decks, emails, and discussions, including all prior ListingStory proposals and the July 17, 2026 email correspondence, none of which shall have binding effect. Exhibit A — Order Form No. 1: Module 1, "The Reveal"

Scope: (1) branded seller intake form with privacy notice, consent, and seller accuracy attestation; (2) Monday.com synchronization of intake records (field mapping agreed at setup); (3) AI drafting engine producing draft listing remarks and social caption per intake, with automated fair-housing word filter and Client approval workflow; (4) branded feature-sheet PDF per intake; (5) branded listing page per property launched through the system (Client bio/team content, property information, photographs via upload link), hosted by Provider; (6) basic listing-page traffic metrics and a shareable activity report.

Onboarding support: white-glove support for Client's first three (3) listings, up to two (2) working sessions each.

Target timeline: production hardening and field mapping in week 1; photo pipeline, analytics, end-to-end sample test, and go-live in week 2 — subject to Client Dependencies (Section 6.3).

Acceptance: the earlier of (a) Client's written approval of a successful end-to-end test with one sample property (intake → Property Story → feature sheet → listing page → Monday.com sync); or (b) five (5) business days after Provider notifies Client the system is live and available for testing, unless Client identifies a specific, reproducible defect in writing. Minor cosmetic items do not delay Acceptance.

Fees: Build fee $3,500 — $1,750 on signature of this Agreement; $1,750 on Acceptance, net 7 days. Monthly service fee $395, commencing on the Acceptance date, billed monthly in advance.

Fair-use assumption: the monthly fee assumes up to fifteen (15) new listings launched through the system per calendar month; sustained volume above this level is addressed by good-faith Order Form amendment.

Initial Term: twelve (12) months from Acceptance, then month-to-month.
